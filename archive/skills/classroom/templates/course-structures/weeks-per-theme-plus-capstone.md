# Course Structure — Weeks per Theme + Capstone

> **Summary (for offering to the user):** Group the weeks into a handful of
> themes (N weeks each), then finish with a hands-on capstone project. Good for
> interest-driven studies (STEM, nature, animals) where each theme is a
> self-contained topic and the capstone ties it together.

## Shape

- Pick the themes (e.g. STEM: force & motion → energy & light → simple machines → weather). Each theme runs a fixed block of weeks.
- Each theme = one mini-unit with its own cover, working through the chosen lesson structure week by week.
- End with a **capstone**: one bigger build/experiment that draws on the whole course (e.g. the soda-rocket finale).

## Worked example

6-month STEM programme: 4 themes × 6 weeks = 24 weeks + 2-week soda-rocket capstone. Or nature study: 4 animal groups × 3 weeks = 12 weeks (optionally a wrap-up week).

## When to use
Subject splits naturally into discrete topics; learner is motivated by a clear sense of "finishing" each theme and building to a finale.
