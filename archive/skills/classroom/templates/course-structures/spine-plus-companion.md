# Course Structure — Spine + Companion

> **Summary (for offering to the user):** Use a strong existing free curriculum
> as the backbone ("spine") and build a custom companion on top that adds
> dyslexia scaffolding, verified videos, note templates, separate answer keys
> and real-world application. Best when the family wants no gaps against a known
> programme without paying for duplication.

## Shape

1. Identify the spine (see `references/curriculum-spines.md`) and confirm it is free to obtain. The family downloads it.
2. Map the companion to the spine's lesson sequence so there are no gaps.
3. The companion adds only what the spine lacks for this learner — typically: slower scaffolding of key concepts, a visual worked example up front, guided note templates, verified video links, varied question formats, a separate answer key, and real-world application.
4. Do **not** recreate what the spine already does well.

## Worked example
12-month English & Maths for the two teens: The Good & The Beautiful Level 6 as the free spine, with a custom dyslexia companion (5-step lessons, confidence stars, Caravan Challenges, separate answer keys).

## When to use
A capable existing curriculum exists and is free; the gap is *fit* (pace, dyslexia support, format) rather than *content coverage*.
