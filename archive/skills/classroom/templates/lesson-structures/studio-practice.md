# Lesson Structure — Studio Practice

> **Summary (for offering to the user):** A skill-acquisition lesson for hands-on
> creative subjects (drawing, art, craft, music). Objectives → warm-up →
> technique demonstration → guided practice with a large practice space →
> reflection, with a running skills checklist and periodic assessment pieces.
> Best when the learner is building a physical technique over many weeks.

## The steps

1. **Objectives** — what the learner will be able to do by the end of the lesson.
2. **Materials** — the short supplies list for this lesson.
3. **Warm-up** — a brief, low-stakes loosener (e.g. a 5-minute free sketch).
4. **Demonstration** — the technique taught, ideally via a short video plus a visual example. Follow the link rules in `references/vetted-video-channels.md`.
5. **Guided practice** — step-by-step application with a large practice space (drawing box / staff lines / work area).
6. **Reflection** — a short prompt, plus the running **skills checklist** ticked off and a date / teacher-initials line.

## Periodic assessment pieces

Over a long course, schedule one or two larger assessment pieces (e.g. a major project across each half of the course) with a brief and a simple marking-criteria table. These suit a course-level certificate and competency report at completion.

## Notes
- Keep the same step order once a format is approved (Agent Invariant 5).
- Suits a 30–45 minute single weekly session.
