# Lesson Structure — Early Learner (Multisensory)

> **Summary (for offering to the user):** A low-text, multisensory lesson for
> emerging readers (roughly ages 5–8). Picture/video hook → say-and-do with
> manipulatives → label or match rather than write → make/craft → a tiny practice
> with answers beneath. Best for early literacy and number sense where writing
> load must stay very low.

## The steps

1. **Hook** — a short picture-led video or image to introduce the focus (a letter, sound, number, creature). Minimal words.
2. **Say & do** — multisensory practice: form letters/numbers with LEGO or tiles, trace, blend by pushing bricks together, count or build. The learning happens in the hands, not on the page.
3. **Label / match** — recognition over production: circle, match, colour, or point-and-label, rather than open writing.
4. **Make** — a quick craft (`templates/blocks/craft-activity.html`) that embodies the concept.
5. **Tiny practice** — one or two short items, with the answer in small font beneath where the learner's file allows it for this age.

## Notes
- Spiral: bring earlier letters/sounds/numbers back into later lessons (pairs with the spiral-units course structure).
- Keep pages visual, spacious, and short. Confidence stars close the lesson.
- Keep the same step order once approved (Agent Invariant 5).
