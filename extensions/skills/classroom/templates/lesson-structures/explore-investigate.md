# Lesson Structure — Explore & Investigate

> **Summary (for offering to the user):** A discovery lesson for science and
> nature. A hook (often a short video) → an investigation (a hands-on experiment
> or an out-and-about field study) → make or record findings → a notebook
> reflection. Pairs naturally with a build/experiment capstone. Best for curious,
> kinetic learners exploring the physical or natural world.

## The steps

1. **Hook** — a short engaging video or a striking question to open the topic. Follow the link rules in `references/vetted-video-channels.md`.
2. **Key facts / concept** — a small set of age-appropriate facts or a labelled diagram (body parts, a cycle, a force), kept light on text.
3. **Investigate** — either a hands-on experiment (with materials and single-step instructions) or a field study to do while travelling, with observation prompts and note-taking boxes.
4. **Make / record** — a craft (`templates/blocks/craft-activity.html`) or a record of results; a real-world challenge (`templates/blocks/real-world-challenge.html`) fits well here.
5. **Reflect** — notebook reflection: what was observed, one question, one thing learned; plus confidence stars.

## Capstone

A unit or course built on this shape ends well with a capstone build or experiment that draws together what was learned (pairs with the themes-plus-capstone course structure).
