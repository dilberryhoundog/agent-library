# Lesson Structure — Timed Session

> **Summary (for offering to the user):** A time-boxed delivery template rather
> than a new content shape — it runs a lesson within fixed minute blocks, adds a
> short spiral-review block, and gives a faster finisher an extension task. Best
> for a regular multi-times-a-week subject (e.g. English or Maths) where two
> learners of slightly different pace work side by side.

## The blocks (example for a 90-minute session)

1. **Warm-up (0–5 min)** — quick recall of 5 items from last session.
2. **Watch (5–25 min)** — video lesson, paused and rewatched freely, at own pace.
3. **Notes (25–30 min)** — key steps and worked examples into the notebook.
4. **Practice (30–55 min)** — the lesson's worksheet / questions.
5. **Spiral review (55–65 min)** — a few questions revisiting earlier concepts.
6. **Reflection (65–75 min)** — the new concept, one question, one thing to remember.
7. **Extension (75–90 min, faster finisher only)** — a matching extra module or a real-world challenge (`templates/blocks/real-world-challenge.html`).

## Notes
- Label sessions **A / B / C** rather than by weekday, so the schedule survives a shifting week.
- Adjust the minute blocks to the subject and session length; keep the block order once approved.
- Combine with any content shape above (it wraps a lesson in timing); it does not replace the lesson's internal steps.
