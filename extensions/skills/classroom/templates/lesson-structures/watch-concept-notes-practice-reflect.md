# Lesson Structure — Watch · Concept · Notes · Practice · Reflect

> **Summary (for offering to the user):** The five-step lesson. A short video
> first, then a visual concept example, guided notes, mixed-format practice, and
> a confidence/reflection close. A strong general-purpose shape for dyslexic
> kinetic learners; works across subjects and ages.

Fill the workbook shell (`templates/documents/workbook-a4.html`) using these steps. Pull the small repeating pieces from `templates/blocks/`.

## The five steps

1. **Watch** — a short video introducing the concept ("watch first"). Use the `templates/blocks/video-library-page.html` style and follow the link rules in `references/vetted-video-channels.md`. Provide note space beside or under it. Omit cleanly when no good video exists.
2. **Key Concept** — a worked visual example before any reading. This is the anchor of the lesson; keep text minimal and show, don't tell.
3. **Guided Notes** — a skeleton outline or chart to complete while/after watching, not blank lines.
4. **Practice** — questions in varied formats (multiple choice, circle, fill-in, match, draw). Include a clearly labelled Working Space. For kinetic learners add the hands-on element here.
5. **Reflect** — close with confidence stars (`templates/blocks/confidence-stars.html`) and, at unit end, the full reflection page (`templates/blocks/reflection-page.html`).

## Optional per-lesson add-ons

- A Real-World Challenge (`templates/blocks/real-world-challenge.html`) on most lessons — applies the concept to the learners current situation.
- A craft / hands-on activity (`templates/blocks/craft-activity.html`) where the subject suits making something.

## Notes

- Every answer in Practice has a matching entry in the separate answer key (`templates/documents/answer-key-a4.html`).
- Keep the same step order and labels across every lesson once a format is approved (Agent Invariant 5).
